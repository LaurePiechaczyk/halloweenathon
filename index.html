Welcome To the House of Horror

It’s getting dark.

And I’m starting to regret agreeing to meet a friend from school I haven’t seen or heard from in 15 years. Every thing will be alright though. We used to do everything together. Then we left school and they dropped off the face of the Earth. Fast forward to 3 weeks ago when they emailed me an invitation to attend their house out in the sticks. Why didn’t I just ignore it.

It’s around 9PM in the middle of winter and I’m late, as always. I’m driving my old sedan through the woods humming along to the tunes on the radio. The road is winding and narrow, lined either side by trees with a dense fog falling reducing visibility to a few cars’ lengths. Not much further to go I think to myself as I turn off the fog lights.

The radio flicks between static and pop songs. I look down and press the buttons on the radio to change the station, but they all seem to have bad signal. Never mind I think as my eyes return to the road.

Two people are in the middle of the road. I’m going to crash right into them. I pull the steering wheel with all my might, praying that I won’t hit them.

It’s too late.

Welcome To the House of Horror

The car slams into the person on the right-hand side and they roll up onto the bonnet and smash the windscreen. “Oh shit” I shout as I take my hands off the steering wheel and cover my face. The car slides off the road and crashes straight into a tree. I blackout.

“I’m not dead” is all I think as I open my eyes are slowly absorb what’s happened.

“Wait, where am I”. I sit up and feel a sharp jolt of pain down my right side and in my jaw. I wipe my mouth on my sleeve and see the deep claret stain I know only too well is blood. I’m in some sort of dimly lit entrance hall with an open plan staircase directly in front of me. On both the left and right hand side are large dark wooden doors set between wall mounted gas lamps and old dusty paintings of people I did not recognise.

I turn around and see large double doors which I assume must be the front door. I reach out and grab the large brass doorhandle and turn it. Locked. I’ll have to find another way out.
